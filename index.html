Hi! I am a fifth-year PhD Candidate at Duke University in the Department of Sociology. I am advised by James Moody (chair), Lynn Smith-Lovin, Craig Rawlings and Martin Ruef. I'm on the job market!

I'm interested in the ways expert authority changes, with particular attention to medical authority. My dissertation hones in on the mental illness self-diagnosis as a case study of authority shifts.

I utilize tools from both quantitative and qualitative social network analysis to uncover systematic evidence of the relational foundations for the transformation or sustainability of authority.

I collaborate on a wide range of projects including adolescent health, political polarization and network methods, always with an orientation toward understanding the source and impact of relationships. I am also interested in developping social network analysis tools that can help new scholars enter the space and make teaching more effective. Check out our network analysis package IDEANet here!
